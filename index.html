200 years of history

At a point that advanced over the sea, later known as Ponta do Calabouço, between beaches Piaçaba and Santa Luzia, in the historic center of Rio de Janeiro, in 1603 the Portugueses built the Fortaleza de Santiago, origin of the architectural ensemble that now houses the National Museum.
